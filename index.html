RESEARCH DOMAIN

Research Overview

A curriculum-aligned personalized e-learning platform integrating four tightly coupled intelligent components.

📚

Research Problem

Existing e-learning platforms remain static, generic, and poorly aligned with curricula. AI tools often hallucinate content, omit critical syllabus topics, and provide limited transparency. Students struggle with motivation and inefficient learning paths that don't reflect actual knowledge levels.

🔬

Research Gap

Consumer streak systems (Snapchat, Reddit, Duolingo) drive engagement but are not curriculum-driven. Existing dashboards provide visualizations but lack reinforcement mechanisms. No system integrates predictive analytics to forecast learner performance with curriculum-aligned streak mechanics.

🎯

Research Objectives

Design and implement a curriculum-aligned personalized e-learning platform that adapts content structure, learning pathways, and micro-learning tasks based on student performance and engagement — without reliance on black-box AI systems. Extract and structure educational content using teacher-guided validation.

⚙️

Methodology

Rule-based, teacher-controlled, and curriculum-aligned approach. NLP extracts and structures content strictly within syllabus scope. Student performance data from quizzes, engagement logs, and streak analytics dynamically adapt learning paths. Streak-based motivation promotes habit formation.

🧩

Four Core Components

The platform integrates four tightly coupled modules, each addressing a specific challenge in personalized learning:

① ECESE — Content Extraction & Structuring Engine ② ILPG — Intelligent Learning Pathway Generator ③ MLDC — Micro-Learning via Daily Challenges ④ PMSAS — Progress Monitoring & Streak Analytics

PROJECT TIMELINE

Milestones

Select an assessment to view details, dates, and marks allocated.

📋 Project Proposal
📅 Weeks 1–4 ⭐ 10 Marks ✓ Completed

Initial project scoping, problem statement, and literature survey. The team defined the four core components (ECESE, ILPG, MLDC, PMSAS) and identified the research gap: the absence of curriculum-aligned streak mechanics in existing e-learning platforms. Supervisor approval obtained by end of Week 4.

🎤 Progress Presentation I
📅 Week 8 ⭐ 15 Marks ✓ Completed

Presentation of individual component architecture and early prototypes. Each member demonstrated their assigned module: Iroshan on ECESE (NLP parser), Ahamed on ILPG (adaptive pathways), Weerasekara on PMSAS (streak tracking), and Ahmed on MLDC (daily challenges). Data collection plans and system architecture diagrams were presented.

🎤 Progress Presentation II
📅 Weeks 14–16 ⭐ 15 Marks ✓ Completed

System integration demonstration. Cross-component data flows were validated, and integration testing between PMSAS and ILPG was showcased. Usability test feedback from Grade 10–11 and undergraduate pilot participants was discussed. The team demonstrated streak-based engagement tracking with real-time teacher dashboards.

📄 Final Assessment
📅 Weeks 20–22 ⭐ 40 Marks ✓ Completed

Submission of the complete Final Report (Project ID: 25-26j-233) covering all components, evaluation results, and commercialization aspects. The platform achieved all functional, non-functional, user, and system requirements. A positive correlation between streak length (days 7–21) and improved quiz performance was confirmed during evaluation.

🎓 Viva
📅 Week 22+ ⭐ 20 Marks ⏳ Pending

Individual oral examination covering each member's contribution, research methodology, and system implementation. Questions will cover PMSAS predictive analytics, ECESE NLP pipeline, ILPG adaptive pathway logic, and the MLDC daily challenge scheduler. Each member expected to defend their individual component in depth.
